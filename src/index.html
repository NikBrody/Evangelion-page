Neon genesis evangelion

Anime that Shaped a Generation

Neon Genesis Evangelion is a Japanese anime series that aired from 1995 to 1996. It tells the story of a group of teenagers who pilot giant robots called Evangelions to fight against monstrous beings called Angels that threaten to destroy humanity. The series combines elements of science fiction, psychology, philosophy, religion, and existentialism, and explores themes such as identity, trauma, depression, and human nature.

Neon Genesis Evangelion is widely regarded as one of the most influential and groundbreaking anime of all time. It has been praised for its complex and original plot, its rich and diverse characters, its stunning animation and music, and its daring and controversial ending. It has also been criticized for its graphic violence, sexual imagery, and religious symbolism. The series has inspired countless works of art, literature, music, film, and video games, and has influenced the genres of mecha, psychological thriller, and post-apocalyptic fiction. It has also spawned several spin-offs, adaptations, and remakes, including the ongoing Rebuild of Evangelion film series.

Neon Genesis Evangelion is more than just an anime. It is a cultural phenomenon that has left a lasting impact on millions of fans around the world. It is a masterpiece that challenges the conventions of storytelling and invites viewers to question their own beliefs and values. It is a legend that continues to resonate with new generations of audiences who discover its beauty and complexity. It is Neon Genesis Evangelion.

Manga

Evangelion is a famous and influential mecha manga. It was created by Yoshiyuki Sadamoto and developed parallel to the original anime series. However, the manga is not a simple adaptation, but an independent work with its own interpretation of characters and events.

The manga tells the story of humanity’s fight against mysterious beings called Angels, who threaten to destroy the Earth. To do this, giant biomechanical robots called Evangelions are created, which can only be piloted by teenagers. The main character is Shinji Ikari, who reluctantly became an Evangelion pilot and got involved in the conflict between Angels and the secret organization NERV. The manga explores themes such as psychology, religion, philosophy and ethics.

Rebuilds of evangelion

Evangelion: 1.0 You Are (Not) Alone

A teenage boy named Shinji Ikari is recruited by a secret organization to pilot a giant robot called Evangelion and fight against monstrous beings called Angels that threaten humanity. He struggles with his father’s expectations, his own fears and the mysteries surrounding the Evangelion project.

Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance

Shinji meets two new Eva pilots: the confident Asuka Langley Shikinami and the mysterious Rei Ayanami. Together, they face more powerful and unpredictable Angels, as well as a rogue Eva unit that awakens from its slumber. Shinji also learns more about his father’s motives and the secrets of the Eva project.

Evangelion: 3.0 You Can (Not) Redo

Shinji wakes up 14 years after a cataclysmic event that he unknowingly triggered. He finds himself in a world where everything has changed: his friends are now enemies, his father is missing and the Eva project is in the hands of a radical faction. He joins forces with a new group of rebels who offer him a chance to undo his mistake and save the world.

Evangelion: 3.0+1.0 Thrice Upon a Time

The final chapter of the rebuild series. Shinji faces his ultimate choice: to accept the reality he lives in or to create a new one. He is confronted by the consequences of his actions and the fate of humanity. He must also reconcile with his father, his friends and himself. The epic battle between the Evas and the Angels reaches its climax, as the fate of the world hangs in the balance.

This is (not) remake

The Rebuild of Evangelion is a series of four animated films that reimagines the original Neon Genesis Evangelion anime series from the acclaimed director Hideaki Anno. The Rebuild films are not a simple remake, but a new interpretation that explores different themes, characters and scenarios in the Evangelion universe. The Rebuild films do not erase or replace the original series, but complement and expand it, offering new insights and perspectives on the story of humanity’s struggle against the Angels.
